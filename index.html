Hypothesis and Methodology

Before diving into creating stories on Melody, it was important to familiarize ourselves with the platform. We started by spending some time exploring Melody's website and gaining a full understanding of its features, functionality and guidelines for creating engaging stories. We also immersed ourselves in the platform's interface so that we would be better equipped to use the tools effectively and create engaging stories in line with Melody's objectives.

Our approach involved focusing our research on the data while keeping a predetermined two hypotheses in mind. This allowed us to formulate questions based on the available data and anticipate potential answers. In this sense, the use of the FRED platform was also crucial, as it gave us very interesting insights in a very schematic way.

The initial phase of our project entailed developing two narrative accounts, known as Melody stories, on the Melody website utilizing knowledge graphs. To initiate this process, we established a Melody/GitHub account. By selecting the "start now" option on the website, we gained access to the tools necessary for crafting our data-driven story.

Step 1. Determine our stories topics

To begin with, we had to choose two topics that combined the theme of music with the region of Emilia-Romagna: “The popularity of musical artists from Emilia-Romagna” and “Rock music in Emilia-Romagna”.
